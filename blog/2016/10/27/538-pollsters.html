Pollsters used by '538' in predicting the US presidential election

27 Oct 2016

TL;DR Summary:

I have a quick look at the polling data used by the `538` website in predicting the USA presidential election results

Polling data on the US election is tidy and available

Like many others around the world, I have been watching with interest the spectacle of the democratic process in the United States of America. One of the most influential and watched sites is fivethirtyeight.com, headed by Nate Silver, author of the excellent popularisation of the craft of statistical time series forecasting The Signal and the Noise. I have no intention of adding substantive commentary to the crowded space of analysis of US voting behaviour (some of which is extremely high quality BTW), but I wanted at least poke around the data.

In pale grey text at the bottom of the election prediction page, fivethirytyeight have a link to “Download CSV of polls”. I used this to get a feel for the different ratings and weights (based on pollster quality plus sample size) fivethirtyeight use for pollsters and came up with the graphic below.
